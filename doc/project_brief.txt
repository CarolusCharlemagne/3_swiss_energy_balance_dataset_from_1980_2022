Description:

Unveil the dynamics of Switzerland's energy landscape with the Swiss Energy Balance Dataset, meticulously recorded from 1980 to 2022. This dataset provides a profound look into the energy production, consumption, and management practices of Switzerland, offering insights into domestic production, import/export, storage, conversion, and more.

Key Aspects of the Dataset:

Temporal Coverage: Spanning over four decades, from 1980 to 2022, providing a long-term view of energy trends.
Detailed Breakdown: Includes annual data on electricity, district heating production, and various energy carriers like wood, coal, petroleum products, and renewable sources.
Sector-Specific Insights: Data is categorized by sectors including households, industry, services, transport, and agriculture.
Column Descriptors: 'Year', 'Category', 'Energy Carrier', and 'TJ (Terajoules)' provide a clear, quantifiable perspective of energy use and production.
Translated for Accessibility: Each column and entry has been carefully translated, inviting a global audience to explore and analyze the data.
Using the Dataset:

License: Shared under the Open Data Commons Attribution License (ODC-By). Attribution is required for all forms of use.
Source Citation: Always include a citation with the author, title, and link to the dataset: Swiss Federal Energy Balance Dataset.
Community Contribution: Users are encouraged to delve into the dataset, analyze trends, and share insights, while respecting the terms of use.
Acknowledgment:

We extend our gratitude to opendata.swiss and the Swiss Federal Office of Energy (SFOE) for upholding transparency and providing this invaluable resource. Their commitment enables informed research, policy-making, and a deeper understanding of energy management in Switzerland.

Embark on a journey of discovery with this rich dataset, and join the global community in exploring the nuances of Switzerland's energy story over the past 42 years!
